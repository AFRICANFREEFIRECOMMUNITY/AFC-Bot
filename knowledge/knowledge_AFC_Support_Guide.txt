1. Platform Overview

The African Freefire Community (AFC) is Africa's premier Free Fire competitive esports platform. It connects players, teams, and tournament organisers across the continent with a strong focus on Nigeria and Sub-Saharan Africa.

The platform is live at:

https://africanfreefirecommunity.com

What You Can Do on AFC

 * Create an account and build your player profile using your Free Fire UID

 * Create or join a team with friends

 * Register for and compete in tournaments and scrims

 * View live leaderboards and match standings

 * Read community news and tournament updates

 * Vote in the annual NFCA (Nigerian Free Fire Community Awards)

 * Purchase Diamond bundles and community coins in the shop

 * List yourself or your team in the Player Transfer Market

 * Get your team sponsored

2. Getting Started Account Creation & Login

Creating an Account

Any Free Fire player can register for free. You will need your Free Fire UID to sign up.

Steps to create an account:

1. Go to the homepage and click the green Create Account button, OR go directly to the Join page.

2. Fill in your Username (your AFC display name up to 40 characters).

3. Enter your Full Name.

4. Enter your Free Fire UID (up to 15 digits this must be unique and accurate).

5. Enter your Email Address.

6. Choose a Password (minimum security requirements apply).

7. Select your Country.

8. Click Submit / Create Account.

9. You will receive a verification email check your inbox and follow the steps in Section 3.

Direct link: africanfreefirecommunity.com ( https://africanfreefirecommunity.com )

++
| 📌 Important |
| |
| Your Free Fire UID must be your real in-game UID this is used to verify your identity in tournaments. |
| |
| Usernames cannot contain spaces and must be unique across the platform. |
| |
| You can only be a member of one team at a time. |
++

Logging In

Steps to log in:

10. Click 'Log In' in the top navigation bar on any public page.

11. A login modal will appear. Enter your In-Game Name or UID and your Password.

12. Click the Login button.

13. You will be redirected to your player dashboard at /home.

Direct link: https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/home

++
| 📌 Tip |
| |
| If you cannot log in, ensure your UID is entered correctly this is your Free Fire in-game UID, not your username. |
| |
| If you forgot your password, see Section 4 Password Reset. |
++

3. Email Verification & OTP

After registering, AFC sends a verification code (OTP) to the email address you used. You must verify your email before you can fully use the platform.

Steps to verify your email:

14. After signing up, check your email inbox for a message from AFC.

15. The email contains a 6-digit verification code.

16. You will be taken to the Email Confirmation screen automatically after signing up.

17. Enter the 6-digit code into the OTP input boxes on screen.

18. Click Verify. Your account will be activated.

++
| 📌 Didn't receive the code? |
| |
| Check your spam/junk folder first. |
| |
| If not there, look for the 'Resend Code' button on the verification screen and click it. |
| |
| You can also use the 'Resend Verification Code' option from the login screen. |
| |
| Codes expire after a set period if yours expired, request a new one. |
++

4. Password Reset

If you have forgotten your password, you can reset it via email.

Steps to reset your password:

19. On the login screen, click 'Forgot Password' or look for the reset option.

20. Enter the email address associated with your AFC account.

21. Click Send Reset Link / Submit.

22. Check your email inbox for a password reset message.

23. Click the link in the email (or enter the reset token if prompted).

24. Enter your new password and confirm it.

25. Click Save / Reset Password.

26. Log in with your new password.

++
| 📌 Note |
| |
| Password reset emails may take a few minutes to arrive. |
| |
| The reset link typically expires after a short period use it promptly. |
| |
| If you do not receive the email, check your spam folder. |
++

7. Teams Joining, Creating & Managing

Teams are the core competitive unit on AFC. Most tournaments are squad-based. You can only be on one team at a time.

Teams page: https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/teams

Browsing Teams

38. Go to https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/teams

39. Use the search bar to search by team name.

40. Filter by 'All Teams' or 'My Team' using the tabs.

41. Each team card shows the team name, logo, number of members, and tier level.

42. Click 'View Team' to see a team's full profile, roster, and stats.

43. Click 'Apply to Join' to send a join request (requires you to be logged in and not already on a team).

Creating a Team

Any registered player who is not already on a team can create one.

44. Log in to your account.

45. Go to https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/teams and click the 'Create Team' button (top right).

46. Fill in your Team Name (unique, up to 60 characters).

47. Enter a Team Tag (short abbreviation, max 5 characters e.g. 'VENT').

48. Upload a Team Logo (optional but recommended).

49. Choose Join Settings: 'Open' (anyone can join) or 'By Request' (members must be approved).

50. Click Create. You are now the Team Owner and Team Captain.

++
| 📌 Team Name Rules |
| |
| Team names must be unique across the platform no two teams can have the same name. |
| |
| Team tags are limited to 5 characters. |
| |
| You become the Team Owner when you create a team ownership can be transferred later. |
++

Inviting Members to Your Team

As a team captain or owner, you can invite players in two ways:

Option A Send a Direct Invite

51. Go to your team page.

52. Click 'Invite Member'.

53. Search for the player by username.

54. Send the invite. The player will receive a notification and can accept or decline.

Option B Generate an Invite Link

55. Go to your team management panel.

56. Click 'Generate Invite Link'.

57. Copy the link and share it with the player you want to invite (via Discord, WhatsApp, etc.).

58. The player opens the link, logs in if needed, and clicks Accept to join.

Joining a Team

Option A Apply to Join (Open Teams)

59. Find the team on https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/teams

60. Click 'Apply to Join' on the team card.

61. If the team is set to 'Open', you join immediately.

62. If set to 'By Request', your application goes to the team captain for approval.

Option B Accept an Invite

63. If a team captain sent you a direct invite, you will see a notification.

64. Go to the invite and click 'Accept'.

65. If you received an invite link, open it in your browser and click 'Accept'.

Managing Your Team Roster

 * To kick a member: Go to team management → Find member → Click Kick

 * To change a member's role: Go to team management → Manage Roster → Edit role

 * To leave a team: Go to your team page → click 'Exit Team' (you cannot leave if you are the only owner)

 * To transfer ownership: Team management → Transfer Ownership → Select new owner

 * To disband a team: Team management → Disband Team (this is permanent and cannot be undone)

9. Leaderboards & Rankings

AFC maintains leaderboards at the per-match level and overall tournament level, as well as a monthly ranking system and quarterly tiering system.

Leaderboards page: https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/a/leaderboards

How Rankings Work
 Leaderboard Type Description

 Match Leaderboard Rankings per individual match based on kills and placement within that match

 Overall Leaderboard Cumulative score across all matches in a tournament determines tournament winner

 Monthly Rankings Updated monthly tracks short-term performance across all tournaments and scrims

 Quarterly Tiering Updated every quarter (season) determines your team's Tier classification (Tier 1, 2, or 3)
How Scores Are Calculated

Scores are based on:

 * Number of kills in the match

 * Final placement/position in the match (1st place = most points)

 * Consistency across multiple matches in a tournament

Team Tier System

Teams are classified into tiers based on their quarterly performance:
 Tier Description

 Tier 1 Highest tier elite teams best benefits, priority event access

 Tier 2 Competitive mid-level teams

 Tier 3 Entry level all new teams start here
Tier is determined by a combination of: Tournament Wins, Prize Money Earned, Total Tournament Kills, Tournament Placements, Finals appearances, Social Media metrics, and Scrim performance.

17. FAQ Common Questions

Account & Login

Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?

A: Go to the login page, click 'Forgot Password', enter your email address, and follow the reset link sent to your inbox. See Section 4 for full steps.

Q: I didn't receive my verification email. What should I do?

A: Check your spam/junk folder. If it's not there, use the 'Resend Code' button on the verification page. See Section 3 for full steps.

Q: I entered the wrong Free Fire UID when signing up. Can I change it?

A: UIDs cannot be self-changed after registration. Contact an AFC admin on Discord at discord.gg/afc to request a correction.

Q: Can I have multiple accounts?

A: No. Each player is only allowed one account. Multiple accounts or account sharing will result in a ban.

Teams

Q: How do I create a team?

A: Log in, go to https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/teams, and click 'Create Team'. Fill in your team name, tag, and settings. See Section 7 for full steps.

Q: Can I be on two teams at once?

A: No. AFC only allows one active team per player. Leave your current team first before joining another.

Q: I sent an invite but the player hasn't received it. What do I do?

A: Try generating an invite link instead (Section 7 Option B). Share the link directly via Discord or WhatsApp.

Q: How do I leave a team?

A: Go to your team page and click 'Exit Team'. Note: if you are the only owner, you must transfer ownership first before leaving.

Q: How do I disband my team?

A: Only the Team Owner can disband. Go to team management and click 'Disband Team'. This is permanent and cannot be undone.

Events & Tournaments

Q: How do I register for a tournament?

A: Log in, find the event at https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/a/events, open the event page, and click Register. For squad events, make sure you are on a team first. See Section 8 for full details.

Q: I registered but didn't get my Discord role. What should I do?

A: First, make sure your Discord account is linked to your AFC profile (Settings → Connect Discord). If it's connected and the role is still missing, contact a moderator on Discord at discord.gg/afc admins have a tool to retry failed role assignments.

Q: How do I get a room code / password for my match?

A: Room codes and passwords are sent to you via Discord notification once your Discord account is linked. If you haven't linked Discord, go to Settings and connect your account now.

Q: Can I enter a tournament without a team?

A: Yes Solo format tournaments allow individual entry. Check the event's Participant Type. Duo and Squad tournaments require a team.

Q: I see an event but the Register button isn't visible. Why?

A: This could mean: (1) registration is closed, (2) the event is full, (3) you don't meet eligibility criteria (e.g. regional restrictions), or (4) it's a private/invite-only event.

Leaderboards & Rankings

Q: Where can I see the leaderboard for a specific tournament?

A: Go to https://africanfreefirecommunity.com/a/leaderboards, search for the event, and click View to see the full leaderboard.

Q: How is my score calculated?

A: Scores are based on kills and final placement in each match. Higher placement (1st = most points) combined with kill count determines your score. See Section 9 for details.

Q: My results don't appear on the leaderboard. What should I do?

A: Results are processed after match screenshots are submitted and verified. If your results are missing after 24 hours, contact an AFC admin on Discord.
