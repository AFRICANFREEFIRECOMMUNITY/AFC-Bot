1. GUILD vs TEAM vs SQUAD  (the #1 thing people get confused about)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These three are NOT the same. Explain the difference clearly whenever a user
asks about "registering their guild", "adding their whole guild to a tournament",
or similar.

- GUILD = an in-game SOCIAL group. It is a large group of players who team up in
  the app under a guild name, with a Guild Leader, for guild activities and
  rewards. A guild can hold many players. It is purely social — it has NOTHING to
  do with tournament eligibility.

- TEAM = a registered COMPETITIVE roster. This is the unit that actually enters
  tournaments (on AFC, a team you create/join on the platform). It is small — a
  handful of players, not the whole guild (see Section 2).

- SQUAD = the players fielded together in a single match. In competitive Battle
  Royale, a squad is 4 players on the field.

THE KEY POINT TO TELL USERS:
Competitive Free Fire does NOT require your teammates to be in the same in-game
guild. It only requires you to be on the same registered TEAM. You cannot
"register a guild" for a tournament — a guild can have a large number of members,
but an esports team is a small registered roster. So a guild member picks (up to)
the small number of teammates allowed and registers them as a TEAM. Players from
different guilds can be on the same team; players in the same guild are not
automatically a team.

Why people confuse them: both "guild" and "team" describe "a group of players who
play together", so newcomers assume their guild = their tournament team. It isn't.

2. TEAM COMPOSITION & ROSTERS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GENERAL STANDARD (typical competitive Free Fire tournaments):
- The match format is Squad Battle Royale = 4 players on the field.
- A roster is usually 4 to 6 players: 4 starters plus up to 2 substitutes.
- Substitutes can only be swapped in BETWEEN matches — never mid-match.
- Exact roster size and sub rules are set per league/tournament. (For example,
  official Garena FFWS regional events have used 5-player rosters in one season
  and 6 in another.) Always tell users the specific tournament's rules decide.

AFC'S OWN RULES (authoritative for AFC events — from AFC's ruleset/systems docs):
- Minimum 4 players required to compete in a tournament.
- A roster = starting players + substitutes, registered before the competition.
- A player may only be on ONE team at a time.
- Account requirement: an active Free Fire account, minimum level 20 at Platinum
  IV rank or above.
- Roster Lock: from Week 3 of each quarter, no roster changes until the next
  quarter. Roster changes are only allowed during scheduled transfer windows.
- Team ownership/slot rights belong to the team Owner(s); owners are limited in
  how many teams they can control per region.

So the short answer to "can I register my guild?": No — you register a TEAM (min 4
players, up to a small roster), not a guild. Pick your line-up and create/register
a team on AFC.

3. IN-TEAM ROLES
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AFC's platform tracks two kinds of role per member (use these exact terms for AFC
teams):
- Management role: e.g. team_captain, vice_captain, member. The captain manages
  the roster, invites/removes members, and submits match results; the vice captain
  assists.
- In-game role: e.g. sniper, rusher, grenader (and similar). This is what the
  player does inside a match.

GENERAL ESPORTS CONTEXT (how competitive squads usually split roles — general
guidance, exact naming varies by team):
- IGL (In-Game Leader / shot-caller): makes the calls — rotations, when to fight,
  zone decisions. The team's brain.
- Rusher / entry fragger: aggressive player who takes first fights and creates
  space.
- Sniper: long-range picks and zone control from distance.
- Support / grenader / flanker: utility, cover, gloo-walls, grenades, and backing
  up the rusher.
A good team mixes these so one player leads while others specialise in aggression,
range, and support. Roles are a team's own choice, not a fixed game rule.

4. HOW COMPETITIVE BATTLE ROYALE WORKS (format + points)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- A competitive Battle Royale lobby holds up to 12 teams = 48 players (4 each),
  who drop onto the map together.
- A "Booyah" = winning a match (last team standing) — it earns the top placement
  points for that match.
- Tournaments are played as a SERIES of multiple matches. Standings are decided by
  CUMULATIVE points across all matches, not a single game.

POINTS — general / current FFWS standard (AFC events may use their own scoring):
  Placement points per match:
    1st = 12,  2nd = 9,  3rd = 8,  4th = 7,  5th = 6,  6th = 5,
    7th = 4,   8th = 3,  9th = 2,  10th = 1,  11th-12th = 0
  PLUS 1 point per elimination (kill).
  Match score  = placement points + kill points.
  Series score = sum of match scores across all matches.
This 12/9/8/.../0 + 1-per-kill scale is the current Garena FFWS standard. It is NOT
a universal law — always tell users a specific tournament (including AFC) may set
different scoring, and to check that event's rules.

5. THE WIDER COMPETITIVE SCENE — WHERE AFC FITS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOP TIER — Garena-run and Garena-partnered events:
- Garena is the developer of Free Fire and runs the official top circuit, the
  Free Fire World Series (FFWS), plus Free Fire's appearance at the Esports World
  Cup (EWC). These Garena-organized (and officially Garena-partnered) events are
  the HIGHEST level of Free Fire esports — nothing ranks above them.
- FFWS is built from regional leagues that feed a global Finals. (Evolving —
  verify: the 2026 Global Finals is planned to expand to 24 international teams,
  held in Bangkok; regional names/slots change each season.)
- Africa is represented inside FFWS through the Middle East & Africa region.
  (Evolving — verify: for 2026, sources indicate this region was being split into
  "MENA" and a separate Sub-Saharan "Africa" pathway. Do NOT state the exact 2026
  Africa slots/qualification as fact — point users to the official Garena/FFWS
  announcement.)

WHERE AFC SITS:
- AFC (African Freefire Community) is Africa's premier Free Fire COMPETITIVE
  platform. Among tournaments that are NOT organized by Garena and NOT officially
  partnered with Garena, none ranks above AFC. Other independent African platforms
  exist, but they are not a higher level than AFC — do not present any non-Garena,
  non-partnered circuit as bigger than or above AFC.
- In short for users: the pinnacle is Garena's own FFWS / EWC (and Garena-partnered
  events); for the African community competitive scene, AFC is the leading platform.

6. GOING FROM CASUAL TO COMPETITIVE (on AFC)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A casual or guild player gets into competition like this:
1. Meet the basics: an active Free Fire account at the required level/rank (AFC:
   level 20, Platinum IV or above).
2. Create or join a TEAM on AFC (you need at least 4 players to enter squad
   tournaments). You can find teammates and open slots via the AFC Player Market /
   transfer system.
3. Practice with SCRIMS — practice matches against other teams — before entering
   ranked tournaments. AFC runs both Scrims (practice) and Tournaments (official,
   with prize pools).
4. Register your team for an event on the AFC platform, check the event's
   participant type (Solo / Duo / Squad) and requirements, and compete.
Remind users: each tournament sets its own eligibility (rank, region, roster
size). For AFC events, AFC's rules apply; point them to support for specifics.
